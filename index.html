Tezuka Osamu Manga Museum

Considered the "god" of the modern styles of anime and manga music, Osamu Tezuka was one of the pioneers in adapting these art forms in the 1940s and 1960s.

Ghibli Museum

Studio Ghibli is best known for producing great animations that include "Howl's Moving Castle," "Princess Mononoke," and "Ponyo." The Studio Ghibli Museum in Tokyo offers an inside look at how these films were handcrafted by Hayao Miyazaki and his team of animators.

Read More

Kyoto International Manga Museum

The Kyoto International Manga Museum, as a library and museum, was established by the City of Kyoto and Kyoto Seika University to "collect, preserve, and display manga materials", as well as conduct research, hold exhibitions, and events on manga culture from around the world..

Read More

Suginami Animation Museum

The Suginami Animation Museum presents the history of Japanese animation, showcases the process of making anime, and showcases a selection of Japanese animation in its theater. Including permanent exhibitions on the history, basics, and future of Japanese anime.

Read More

Pokemon Center

The Pokémon Center in Japan sells a variety of Pokemon merchandise at many locations, but the Tokyo location, called MEGA TOKYO is the largest and offers the biggest events and attractions.

Read More

Kiddy land

Kiddy Land is a store that has been serving more than 60 years in Japan with the latest games, anime, manga and all forms of children's entertainment in Japan. With several locations across the country, this popular local attraction is a great destination for gifts for kids and gifts for all ages..

Read More

Rare Mandarak Books in Nakano

Nakano district in Tokyo is also a popular area for anime and manga fans. Along the Broadway shopping street in Nakano, there are many stores selling manga and anime products. Mandarake stands out in that it specializes in reselling manga with a network of stores across the country that share rare comics between them.

Read More

Akihabara

Akihabara is well-known for being a center of Japanese otaku ("the cheerleader") heritage, and there are many stores selling Japanese video games, anime, and manga in the area. The Tokyo Anime Center is located on the fourth floor of the UDX building, and there are plenty of arcades and shops throughout the neighborhood.
